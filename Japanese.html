Japanese Food History


Japan cuisine was developed off of the influence of China. The culture valued the visual appeal of food, and it also valued that taste be brought out to the fullest. Rice, wheat and soybeans are all staples in Japanese culture, in many ways since the Japanese were Buddhists; they wouldn’t eat meat or fish. Shortly after, however, the fish ban was revoked, but the meat ban stayed the same. The Japanese used very little spice and got their meat from fishing, which has become yet another staple in Japanese culture. The Japanese ate fish in every one of their meals, and oil and fat were generally avoided due to the healthy lifestyle of the Japanese. All of these factors were key in making Japanese cuisine what it is today, and it’s also why Japanese cuisine is so vastly different than other east Asian cuisines. Preserved fish picked up, and before long sushi was invented. Putting raw fish in boiled rice triggered something called lactic acid fermentation, which prevents the spread of bacteria on the fish. Recently, a new ruler declared eating meat was legal, since he wanted to embrace western culture, and now people from japan eat meat. However, fish still dominates the Japanese market.

Japanese Food now

Now Japanese food is about combining the staple food, rice, with other dishes. Since Japanese tradition says that different foods shouldn't touch each other, each dish does not touch, so they are usually given their respective bowls or plates. When there is not enough room, or there are not enough plates, the food is separated by some form of non-toxic material, such as leaves. Due to this, placing main dishes on top of rice is usually frowned upon. Rice is known as the staple food, but seafood could be considered just as well-known; seafood is present in almost every single dish the Japanese make. For example, sushi and takoyaki, which both involve seafood. The Japanese also care a LOT about freshness - so much so that they sometimes eat the food alive. Overall, Japanese food is fresh, and centered around rice and seafood.















Fun Facts

The Japanese consider rice "soiled" if you put a dish on top of it!


In Japan, you can eat live octopus!
